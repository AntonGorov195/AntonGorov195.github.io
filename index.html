About me

Hi, I'm a recent graduate from HIT university and a proud lover of programming. My journey started back in Unity game rngine 5.4 (2016). This was a great time. But my joy came from programming, not the games themselves. It wasn't about creating things, it was about solving problems. It was my love for learning and a deep sense of curiosity which lead my here.


However, as a game engine, it enforced a specific workflow. It wasn't a bad one per say, but I wanted more. So of course, I wanted to know why it worked the way it did. This is how most people focused on the surface level things. This kinda makes sense considering Unity is closed source.


One thing I did see are discussions around raw pointers. How the are scary and cause so many bugs and such. So naturally I dug deeper. This is how I learn about manual memory existing, virtual memory, function pointers. At that point I still didn't dare to venture away from Unity. I tried Godot but it wasn't all too different from Unity.


Time has passed. I begun going to university. I met great people; learned a lot of new things. It is hard for me to construct a timeline due to how many new things I was learning at once. Of course, it was more than the courses. I programmed small experiments at home. I discovered new ways to programs. New paradigms uncovered.


Now I'm here. I've on a long journey to get here. Now I'm looking for job opportunities. If you have one, please contact me at antongorov195@gmail.com.
